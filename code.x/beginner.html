How We Discover Them ‚Äî Transit Method

When a planet passes in front of its star, it blocks a tiny fraction of light. Measuring the dip tells us the planet's size; timing the repeats gives its orbital period.

Approximate brightness curve (deeper dip = larger planet)
Depth: 100.0%

During a transit, the fractional drop is roughly (Rp/Rs)^2, where Rp is planet radius and Rs is star radius.

Why it works

  • Precision photometry: space telescopes detect changes smaller than 1/10,000.
  • Periodic signals: repeating dips point to orbits rather than random noise.
  • Follow-up: radial-velocity confirms mass; spectra probe atmospheres.

What is a Light Curve?

A light curve is a plot of star brightness vs. time (or orbital phase). During transit a planet blocks starlight creating a dip. With a model fit we estimate radius and more.
